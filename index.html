Royal Chocolat is one of the recipes you should know when you want to take the CAP Pâtissier. It is also one of the favorite recipes of the French for Christmas and Easter, in particular because it is one of the simplest desserts. We can find this recipe under several names, like "Le Royal" (quite simply) or "Le Trianon", but generally, we stay on the same basis of composition, namely a soft cookie (usually a dacquoise), a crunchy praline and a chocolate mousse.

Personally, what I prefer in this recipe is above all the delicacy brought by the crisp. It is obtained through flaky feuilletine which can easily be obtained by roughly crumbling commercially available lace pancakes. Even if it is not complicated to make, Royal Chocolat is not a recipe that we can make at the last moment, as we would for example make a chocolate fondant.
